# Evidence-Based Diet for Cardiometabolic Health, Longevity, and Cancer Risk Reduction
### Version 2 — Calibrated to ~1,600 kcal/day

---

## Scientific Foundation

This plan synthesises five converging dietary frameworks, each with the strongest available evidence:

| Goal | Evidence Base | Effect Size |
|---|---|---|
| Lower LDL cholesterol | **Portfolio Diet** (Jenkins et al., multiple RCTs) | −17% LDL, −32% CRP |
| Lower blood pressure | **DASH + low sodium** | −8.9/−4.5 mmHg systolic/diastolic |
| Blood sugar / T2D prevention | **Low-glycemic Mediterranean** | −58% T2D incidence (DPP trial) |
| Longevity / anti-aging | **AHEI + caloric moderation** | 86% greater odds of healthy aging at 70+ |
| Cancer risk reduction | **Healthy plant-based diet** (2025 Lancet meta-analysis) | Significant reduction in all-cause and cancer mortality |

The plan is a **Mediterranean-Portfolio Diet hybrid**: plant-forward, rich in soluble fiber, polyphenols, omega-3s, and soy protein; low sodium; low saturated fat.

---

## Calorie Target and Metabolic Context

This plan is calibrated to **~1,600 kcal/day** — appropriate for individuals with metabolic adaptation, insulin resistance, or a measured TDEE significantly below what standard equations predict. The Portfolio Diet, DASH, and Mediterranean frameworks are all fully achievable at this intake; it requires precision with oils, grains, and nuts, but does not compromise any therapeutic target.

**Computed daily average: ~1,640–1,680 kcal** (verified ingredient by ingredient). Individual days range from ~1,510 to ~1,800 kcal depending on protein source. Adjust walnuts ±5g or EVOO ±3g per meal to fine-tune.

The most likely cause of a low measured TDEE with significant exercise is **insulin resistance** — chronically elevated insulin suppresses lipolysis regardless of calorie arithmetic. This diet directly addresses that mechanism through: low glycemic load, high soluble fiber, berberine (AMPK activation), time-restricted eating, and elimination of all refined carbohydrates. Weight loss, when it comes, should improve all five therapeutic goals simultaneously.

Consider asking your doctor for a **fasting insulin level and HOMA-IR score** alongside the standard lipid panel — these are not part of a routine panel but give the clearest picture of what's actually happening metabolically.

## Structural Principles

The following changes from a standard Mediterranean diet are intentional and evidence-driven:

**Removed from the base plan** (available as considered indulgences — see end of document):
- All bread, toast, wraps, pita, pasta, couscous
- Brown rice (replaced by barley at 2 dinners; otherwise legumes fill the role)
- Eggs (removed for maximum LDL-lowering consistency; whole eggs reintroduce dietary cholesterol)
- Feta and all other cheeses
- Smoked and cured fish (sodium)
- All poultry (replaced by legumes; plant proteins outperform animal proteins on every stated goal)
- Honey and all other added sugars
- Banana (replaced by berries; lower GI, higher polyphenol density)
- Whole wheat wraps and commercial pita (processed wheat products, higher GI than labelled)

**Calibrated for 1,600 kcal:**
- EVOO: **5g in soups and stews; 8g in roasted or pan-cooked dishes.** Measure — do not pour.
- Nuts: **25g walnuts daily as snack** (reduced from the Portfolio Diet's 45g ideal; the supplement protocol compensates for the therapeutic gap)
- Fish portions: **130g** (down from 160g)
- Legume portions: specified as cooked weight throughout to avoid ambiguity
- No grain carbohydrates at lunch (legumes and vegetables carry the load)
- Barley appears at 2 dinners (40g dry each) as a secondary beta-glucan source

## Cooking Notes

**The garlic rule — applies to every recipe:** Chop or crush garlic. **Wait 10 minutes before adding to heat.** Allicin (the cardiovascular-active compound) only forms after the enzyme alliinase is released by breaking cell walls — and that enzyme is destroyed by immediate heat. Waiting 10 minutes after chopping roughly doubles the bioavailable allicin.

**Oil is medicine, not seasoning:** The EVOO amounts are therapeutic precision doses, not estimates. Use a kitchen scale or a measuring spoon. 5g ≈ 1 level teaspoon. 8g ≈ just under 2 teaspoons.

**Turmeric always with black pepper:** Every recipe that uses turmeric should include black pepper in the same dish. Piperine increases curcumin bioavailability by approximately 20×.

**Capers:** Always rinse well under running water for 30 seconds — removes ~70% of sodium.

**Olives:** Always rinse before eating — same reason.

**Miso:** Use sweet white (shiro) miso only — lowest sodium of all miso varieties. Maximum 8g per day.

**Tamari:** Always dilute 50/50 with water before using. This halves the sodium without noticeably affecting flavour in a cooked dish.

**Vegetable stock:** Homemade (no salt) or certified no-sodium-added carton only. "Low-sodium" commercial stock still contains 300–500mg per cup.

**Flaxseed:** Ground only — whole seeds pass through largely undigested. Buy pre-ground or grind weekly and store in the freezer.

## Daily Supplement Protocol

Listed from strongest to most emerging evidence. **Consult your physician before starting, particularly berberine if on any diabetes medication (additive glucose-lowering effect), blood thinners, or statins.**

| Supplement | Dose | Timing | Evidence and Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Psyllium husk** | 10g in 250ml water | Before breakfast | Strong RCT evidence: −7% LDL, improves glycaemic control; contributes ~7g viscous fiber toward the 20g daily target |
| **Plant sterols** | 2g | With main meal | Strong: −12% LDL; take as a supplement capsule or use a phytosterol-enriched spread (e.g., Benecol) |
| **Omega-3 (EPA+DHA)** | 2–3g combined | With dinner | Strong: lowers triglycerides, anti-inflammatory, cardiovascular; reduce dose on days eating oily fish |
| **Vitamin D3** | 2,000–4,000 IU | With breakfast | Very common deficiency; evidence for metabolic health, immune function, cancer risk reduction — check serum level before high-dose |
| **Vitamin K2 (MK-7)** | 100–200 mcg | With D3 | Directs calcium to bones rather than arteries; synergistic with D3 for cardiovascular protection |
| **Magnesium glycinate** | 300–400mg | At bedtime | Lowers blood pressure, improves insulin sensitivity, supports sleep and energy |
| **Berberine** | 500mg × 2/day | With 2 meals | Strong meta-analysis evidence: −10–15% LDL, −15–20% fasting glucose; activates AMPK — the same pathway as metformin |
| **CoQ10 (Ubiquinol)** | 100–200mg | With a fatty meal | Heart muscle energy, antioxidant; especially important if taking statins, which deplete CoQ10 |
| **Curcumin + piperine** | 500mg curcumin | With any meal | Broad anti-inflammatory; the piperine in the supplement increases absorption 20× — same effect as pairing turmeric with black pepper in food |
| **NMN** *(optional)* | 250–500mg | Morning | NAD+ precursor; growing clinical evidence for energy metabolism and cellular aging; still emerging and expensive |

## Considered Indulgences

These are foods removed from the base plan. Each entry notes what it costs against the therapeutic goals and when it is reasonable to include it.

**Low cost — add freely:**
- **Additional raw vegetable volume** at any meal: near-zero calories, pure benefit
- **Extra berries** (50–100g): ~25–50 kcal, pure benefit
- **Kimchi or sauerkraut (30g, unseasoned variety)**: ~10 kcal, adds microbiome diversity and anti-inflammatory compounds; excellent condiment for the stir-fry or Buddha bowl days

**Modest cost — include once or twice a week:**
- **Dark chocolate, 85%+ cacao (15–20g)**: ~90–120 kcal. At this percentage, polyphenols, magnesium, and flavonoids make this a genuine health food rather than an indulgence. Has evidence for modest blood pressure and insulin sensitivity benefit.
- **1 slice rye sourdough (60g)**: ~120–140 kcal, moderate GI (~50–55). The most defensible bread option — dense, fermented, low-processed. Use to replace one dinner's vegetable bulk rather than adding on top.
- **Feta (15g) in the Saturday mezze**: ~55 kcal, ~2g saturated fat, ~280mg sodium. Small enough that it does not materially undermine either the cholesterol or BP targets at this portion.

**Real cost — occasional treats, maximum 1×/week:**
- **One whole egg** (hard-boiled in the Wednesday Niçoise): +70 kcal, ~186mg dietary cholesterol. One egg per week is unlikely to materially move LDL for most people, and the yolk contains choline (important for neurological health). If you are monitoring LDL closely, skip it.
- **A glass of red wine (150ml)**: ~125 kcal. Polyphenols and resveratrol have some cardiovascular evidence, though the evidence has weakened considerably under closer review. If you drink, one glass with dinner once a week is the limit consistent with all stated goals.
- **Brown rice (60g dry) added to a lighter dinner** (e.g., Tuesday tempeh): ~208 kcal. Acceptable glycemic impact when combined with tempeh and the large vegetable volume, but barley is always preferable.

**Avoid even occasionally:**
- Any refined wheat products (white bread, pasta, pastries, crackers)
- Any processed or cured meat (bacon, sausage, deli meats, smoked salmon at full portions)
- Fruit juice (liquid sugar without the fruit's fiber; high glycemic spike)
- Any sweetened yogurt or flavored soy products
- Coconut oil or palm oil (high saturated fat with no offsetting benefit)

## What the Evidence Predicts This Plan Achieves

Assuming consistent adherence and the full supplement protocol:

- **LDL cholesterol:** Portfolio Diet components (oats, barley, beans, soy, nuts, plant sterols) can reduce LDL by 15–25%; berberine adds a further 10–15%. Combined reduction of **25–35%** is plausible — comparable to a low-dose statin.
- **Triglycerides:** Elimination of refined carbohydrates + omega-3 supplementation + 4× weekly oily fish: expected reduction of **20–30%**.
- **Blood pressure:** DASH-consistent mineral profile (high potassium, magnesium, calcium; low sodium) + EVOO + magnesium supplement: expected systolic reduction of **8–14 mmHg**.
- **Fasting glucose and insulin resistance:** Low-GI carbohydrates throughout, high viscous fiber, legume base, berberine (AMPK activation): **significant reduction in fasting glucose**; 46–58% reduction in T2D progression risk if applied at prediabetes stage (DPP trial data).
- **Systemic inflammation (CRP):** Mediterranean pattern + curcumin + omega-3: expected reduction **20–40%**.
- **Cancer risk:** Daily cruciferous vegetables (sulforaphane), consistent fiber (colorectal protection), lycopene (tomatoes), absence of red and processed meat, polyphenol density — consistent with the 2025 Lancet meta-analysis showing significant reduction in cancer mortality for this dietary pattern.
- **Metabolic aging (mTOR/IGF-1 pathway):** Plant protein dominance over animal protein, caloric moderation, absence of high-methionine foods (red meat), polyphenol load (berries, EVOO, green tea) — all mechanistically linked to slower metabolic aging in both epidemiological and basic science literature.

> **Important disclaimer:** This is evidence-based nutritional information, not personalised medical advice. Share this plan with your physician before starting, particularly the berberine (additive glucose-lowering effect — relevant if on any diabetes medications or blood thinners) and before beginning vitamin D above 2,000 IU (check serum 25-OH-D level first). Ask specifically for a fasting insulin level and HOMA-IR score alongside the standard lipid panel — these give the clearest picture of insulin resistance and are not included in a routine blood panel.
