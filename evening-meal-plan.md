# Cardiometabolic Evening Meal Plan
## One Meal · ~1,500 kcal · 8 PM

Companion document to `cardiometabolic-diet-plan.md`. All eating concentrated in a single evening meal, preceded by a 20-hour fast. The three-course structure — starter, main, finisher — delivers every therapeutic ingredient without pretending you will eat porridge at 7 AM.

---

## How This Works

**The fast** runs from roughly 1 AM to 8 PM. Coffee with a small amount of milk through the day is fine — the insulin response is negligible. Two cups of green tea during the day (morning, early afternoon) for the EGCG. Nothing else.

**Before sitting down:** 10g psyllium husk stirred into 250ml water, drunk immediately. This delivers ~7g of the day's soluble fiber target before the meal begins and measurably blunts the postprandial glucose response to everything that follows.

**While cooking — the aperitif:** Walnuts, 25g. Eat these over 20 minutes while the soup is on. This is the Portfolio Diet's daily nut requirement, the day's ALA contribution alongside the flaxseed in the finisher, and a mild appetite signal before the main event. A glass of sparkling water is the appropriate accompaniment.

**The three courses:**
- **Starter** (~400–500 kcal): A soup, salad, or bowl. Usually carries the legume and soy protein load.
- **Main** (~500–650 kcal): Fish or plant protein with roasted or wilted vegetables. Two evenings include a small barley portion for secondary beta-glucan.
- **Finisher** (~270–400 kcal): Oat-based. This is the beta-glucan delivery — the single most evidence-backed LDL-lowering food component. Served as a small dessert course. On most evenings it is a simple bowl of rolled oats with warm soy milk and berries. On Saturday it becomes buckwheat galettes. Either way it is a legitimate thing to eat after dinner and not a punishment.

**After the meal:** Nothing except water and herbal tea. The previous bedtime snack (yogurt and protein powder) is retired — the finisher replaces it. If genuine hunger arrives at midnight, 15g walnuts (~100 kcal) are acceptable. Protein powder close to sleep is specifically counterproductive: the insulin spike at peak melatonin blunts overnight fat oxidation and growth hormone secretion.

**Daily total:** ~1,450–1,650 kcal depending on the evening. The walnuts and finisher are the adjustable levers if you want to push below 1,500 on a given night.

---

## Standing Principles

**Garlic:** Chop or crush. Rest 10 minutes before adding to heat. Allicin only forms after the enzyme is released from the broken cell wall, and that enzyme is destroyed by immediate heat. This applies to every recipe below.

**EVOO:** Measured. 5g (one level teaspoon) in soups and stews. 8g (just under two teaspoons) for roasting and pan work. This is the one place in this kitchen where precision serves health rather than anxiety.

**Turmeric and black pepper:** These travel together. Piperine increases curcumin absorption approximately 20-fold. Any dish that takes turmeric naturally should have black pepper in the same pan.

**Capers and olives:** Always rinsed. Removes roughly 70% of the sodium.

**Tamari:** Diluted 1:1 with water before use. Barely noticeable in a cooked dish; halves the sodium.

**Soy milk in the finisher:** Not optional decoration. It delivers 5–6g of soy protein at the end of every evening, topping up whatever edamame, tofu, or tempeh appeared in the main meal.

## The Week

Gram quantities are given only where they do therapeutic or caloric work. Everything else — aromatics, vegetables, herbs, spices — is to taste and appetite. Season as the tradition demands.

---

### MONDAY
*Indian → Atlantic · ~1,480 kcal*

---

**Pre-dinner — Walnuts** (25g · ~163 kcal)

---

**Starter — Masoor Dal with Spinach and Edamame** (~490 kcal · ~15g soy protein)
*Indian in spirit. The whole world makes lentil soup with spinach; this version sits closest to a saag dal, with a Levantine squeeze of lemon at the end.*

Anchors: red lentils (70g dry), edamame cooked (80g, stirred in for the final 3 minutes)
EVOO: 5g to bloom spices

Soften onion, carrot, celery. Add the rested garlic. Bloom cumin, turmeric, black pepper, and smoked paprika briefly in the oil — 30 seconds, no longer. Add the lentils, tomatoes, and enough water to cover generously. Simmer 20 minutes until the lentils collapse into the broth. Wilt spinach through. Stir in edamame. Finish hard with lemon juice and fresh coriander if you have it.

---

**Main — Baked Salmon with Roasted Broccoli and Sweet Potato** (~510 kcal)
*No particular tradition — just good fish cookery.*

Anchors: salmon fillet (130g), broccoli (200g), sweet potato (120g, cubed)
EVOO: 8g

Mix the rested garlic into the oil. Toss sweet potato first — it needs the most time. Add broccoli halfway through. Salmon goes in for the final 12–15 minutes, seasoned with lemon, black pepper, turmeric, and dill or parsley. The broccoli should have some char; the salmon should be just opaque.

---

**Finisher — Bircher with Soy Yogurt and Berries** (~270 kcal · ~10g soy protein)
*Assembled during the day; served cold.*

Rolled oats (45g), soy yogurt (100g), soy milk (120g), mixed berries (100g), ground flaxseed (12g). Combine the night before or morning of. By evening it has thickened to a loose, creamy consistency. No sweetener.

---

### TUESDAY
*Provençal → Indonesian · ~1,400 kcal*

---

**Pre-dinner — Walnuts** (25g · ~163 kcal)

---

**Starter — Salade Niçoise with Edamame** (~440 kcal · ~9g soy protein)
*Classic Niçoise, minus the egg. The edamame is a structural substitution — same role, better protein profile.*

Anchors: sardines in spring water drained (120g), edamame cooked (80g)
EVOO: 8g

The standard Niçoise build: greens, blanched green beans, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, thin-sliced red onion. Sardines broken over the top. Olives (rinsed) and capers (rinsed) as seasoning. Edamame scattered through. Dress with EVOO, red wine vinegar, a little Dijon mustard. This is already a complete and civilised dish; it needs nothing added.

---

**Dinner — Tempeh Stir-Fry** (~490 kcal · ~22g soy protein)
*Indonesian fermented soy in a Chinese-Japanese idiom. The rice is gone — the vegetable volume compensates.*

Anchors: tempeh (130g)
Sesame oil: 5g (added off heat)
Tamari: 8g diluted 1:1 with water

High heat, quick work. Tempeh first, cut into cubes or slices, until it colours. Add garlic (rested), ginger, then broccoli, red pepper, sugar snap peas, bok choy in order of density. The tamari-vinegar-ginger sauce goes in at the end; sesame oil after you've taken it off the heat. Scatter sesame seeds. Serve immediately — this dish doesn't wait.

---

**Finisher — Oat Porridge with Berries** (~270 kcal · ~6g soy protein)

Rolled oats (45g), soy milk (200g), mixed berries (100g), ground flaxseed (12g). Cook the oats in the soy milk; top with cold berries and flaxseed. Cinnamon if you like.

---

### WEDNESDAY
*Spanish → Mexican · ~1,440 kcal*

---

**Pre-dinner — Walnuts** (25g · ~163 kcal)

---

**Starter — Warm Chickpea and Roasted Vegetable Salad** (~490 kcal · ~7g soy protein)
*A Spanish ensalada templada at heart, though it crosses easily into Levantine territory depending on your spice hand. The avocado is Californian.*

Anchors: cooked chickpeas (120g), edamame cooked (60g), avocado (60g)
EVOO: 5g

Roast zucchini and red pepper at high heat until they colour — this matters, the caramelisation is the flavour. Toss warm with chickpeas, edamame, cherry tomatoes, arugula, and thinly sliced red onion. Dress with EVOO, lemon juice, smoked paprika, cumin, turmeric, black pepper. Lay avocado over the top. Fresh parsley or mint.

---

**Main — Black Bean and Cauliflower Chili** (~535 kcal · ~2g soy protein)
*Mexican in structure; the cauliflower is a contemporary addition that works. Season as you would any serious chili negro.*

Anchors: black beans (60g dry, soaked overnight), cauliflower (200g)
EVOO: 5g

Build the base: onion, garlic (rested), red pepper, then the spices — chili, cumin, smoked paprika, turmeric, black pepper. Add soaked beans, tomatoes, enough water to cover. Simmer until beans are fully tender, 45–60 minutes. Add cauliflower florets for the final 15 minutes — they should retain some texture. Finish with lime. Top with sliced avocado (40g) and a spoonful of plain soy yogurt.

---

**Finisher — Oat Porridge with Berries** (~270 kcal · ~6g soy protein)

As Tuesday.

---

### THURSDAY
*Japanese-adjacent → Provençal · ~1,510 kcal*

---

**Pre-dinner — Walnuts** (25g · ~163 kcal)

---

**Starter — Roasted Brussels Sprouts, Edamame and Chickpea Bowl** (~520 kcal · ~20g soy protein)
*No precise tradition — this is the idiom of the Japanese teishoku, the grain bowl as a composed meal. The tahini dressing is Middle Eastern but it works everywhere.*

Anchors: Brussels sprouts roasted (150g), edamame cooked (100g), cooked chickpeas (80g), pumpkin seeds (15g)
Tahini: 8g
EVOO: 5g

Roast Brussels sprouts hard and fast — high heat, some charring on the cut faces is correct. Massage kale with a few drops of the EVOO and a pinch of salt until it softens. Dress tahini with lemon juice, garlic, water to loosen. Compose: kale as the base, sprouts, chickpeas, edamame, cherry tomatoes, pumpkin seeds, tahini dressing poured over. Turmeric and black pepper somewhere in here.

---

**Main — Baked Mackerel with Provençal Vegetables and Barley** (~600 kcal)
*The ratatouille vegetables are classic Provençal; mackerel is the most omega-3-rich fish you can buy. The barley is Northern European but it doesn't conflict.*

Anchors: mackerel fillet (130g), pearl barley (40g dry)
EVOO: 8g

Cook the barley separately in unsalted water. Roast zucchini, eggplant, red onion, and cherry tomatoes with the EVOO, rested garlic, and abundant fresh thyme and oregano. Mackerel goes in for the final 15 minutes — it is an oily fish and wants only lemon, black pepper, and heat. Plate barley alongside the vegetables; mackerel on top.

---

**Finisher — Bircher with Soy Yogurt and Berries** (~270 kcal · ~10g soy protein)

As Monday.

---

### FRIDAY
*Californian → Scottish-Nordic · ~1,515 kcal*

---

**Pre-dinner — Walnuts** (25g · ~163 kcal)

---

**Starter — Tofu, Avocado and Chickpea Bowl** (~490 kcal · ~20g soy protein)
*This is California health-food cooking, unapologetically. It is also genuinely good.*

Anchors: firm tofu (150g), cooked chickpeas (80g), avocado (70g)
EVOO: 5g

Press the tofu dry and cut into cubes. Pan-fry in the EVOO until golden on two sides — do not rush this, the colour is the flavour. Cool slightly. Combine with chickpeas, cherry tomatoes, cucumber, baby spinach, and thin-sliced red onion. Dress with lemon, black pepper, turmeric. Scatter nutritional yeast over everything — it adds a savoury, cheesy register that anchors the dish. Avocado on top.

---

**Main — Rainbow Trout with Barley, Wilted Kale and Leeks** (~595 kcal)
*Scottish or Nordic in character — trout, barley, kale, leeks are the four pillars of cold-water northern cooking. Clean, simple, unfussy.*

Anchors: rainbow trout fillet (130g), pearl barley (40g dry)
EVOO: 8g

Cook the barley in unsalted water or no-salt stock. Soften leeks in the EVOO until they are completely tender — this takes longer than you think. Add peas, then kale, until the kale wilts through. Trout: season with lemon, black pepper, dill. Pan-fry skin-side down for 3–4 minutes, flip for 1 minute. It is done before you think it is. Pile barley and vegetables on the plate; trout alongside.

---

**Finisher — Oat Porridge with Berries** (~270 kcal · ~6g soy protein)

As Tuesday.

---

### SATURDAY
*Lebanese → Moroccan · ~1,650 kcal · the most generous evening of the week*

---

**Pre-dinner — Walnuts** (25g · ~163 kcal)

---

**Starter — Lebanese Mezze: Cauliflower Tabbouleh, Hummus, Edamame** (~440 kcal · ~9g soy protein)
*The tabbouleh is cauliflower rather than bulgur — lower GI, more cruciferous vegetables, indistinguishable once the herbs are in. The hummus is made from scratch or bought without added starch or seed oil.*

Anchors: cooked chickpeas for hummus (80g), cauliflower riced (100g), edamame cooked (80g)
EVOO: 8g (split between hummus and dressing)
Tahini: 8g (in hummus)

Tabbouleh: raw riced cauliflower — do not cook it — tossed with enormous amounts of flat-leaf parsley (40g), mint, diced tomato, cucumber, lemon juice, a thread of EVOO. The cauliflower is a textural background; the parsley is the point. Hummus: blitz chickpeas, tahini, garlic, lemon, EVOO, water until very smooth. Edamame served alongside or scattered through. Olives (rinsed) and raw vegetables as they like.

---

**Main — Moroccan Chickpea and Butternut Squash Tagine** (~610 kcal)
*A Moroccan dish that requires no apology for not containing lamb. The spice profile is the point, and it is complete without meat.*

Anchors: cooked chickpeas (150g), butternut squash (150g)
EVOO: 8g

Build the chermoula base: onion, rested garlic, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, ginger, turmeric, black pepper — all bloomed in the EVOO. Add chickpeas, squash, zucchini, tomatoes. Preserved lemon rind (rinsed), harissa if you use it. Enough water or no-salt stock to braise gently, 30–40 minutes, until the squash is very tender. The sauce should be thick and aromatic. Finish with cilantro, parsley, a few pine nuts (10g) toasted, and pomegranate seeds (25g) — these are not decoration; the pomegranate acid balances the spice.

---

**Finisher — Buckwheat Galettes with Soy Yogurt and Blueberries** (~400 kcal · ~7g soy protein)
*French galettes bretonnes in spirit, built from buckwheat and oats rather than pure buckwheat flour. The soy yogurt and berries make this unambiguously a dessert.*

Buckwheat flour (40g), rolled oats blended fine (20g), flax egg (12g ground flaxseed + 45ml water, rested 5 min), soy milk (80g), cinnamon. Cook in a measured 5g EVOO in a hot pan — thin, lacy-edged, slightly crisp. Serve with plain soy yogurt (100g) and blueberries (100g).

---

### SUNDAY
*Tuscan → Languedoc · ~1,590 kcal · fully plant-based*

---

**Pre-dinner — Walnuts** (25g · ~163 kcal)

---

**Starter — Ribollita** (~495 kcal · ~8g soy protein)
*Tuscan peasant cooking — literally "re-boiled." A white bean and kale soup so thick it is closer to a stew. The silken tofu disappears completely into the broth and is invisible to everyone at the table.*

Anchors: cooked cannellini beans (130g), silken tofu (80g, stirred in at the end)
EVOO: 5g

The classic build: soften onion, carrot, celery, garlic (rested) in the EVOO. Add tomatoes, beans, kale, zucchini, enough water to cover. Thyme, rosemary, a bay leaf. Simmer 30 minutes. Stir silken tofu in off the heat — it dissolves into the broth adding protein and body. Finish with nutritional yeast (12g) for the savouriness that Parmesan used to provide. This soup is better the next day, if there is any.

---

**Main — Cassoulet-Style Lentil and Roasted Vegetable Braise** (~580 kcal · ~8g soy protein)
*The Languedoc cassoulet without the confit duck — which is to say, a very good bean braise. Add lentils to the traditional white bean base and you have something older and more honest than the dish it's named after.*

Anchors: green lentils (55g dry), cooked cannellini beans (80g), edamame cooked (60g)
EVOO: 8g

Roast eggplant, zucchini, red pepper, and red onion separately at high heat — they need their own time and space to caramelise properly. Meanwhile, build the braise: rested garlic, smoked paprika, turmeric, black pepper, tomatoes, lentils, water. Simmer 25 minutes until lentils are just tender. Fold in cannellini and edamame. Combine with the roasted vegetables. Finish with lemon, fresh thyme, nutritional yeast (10g). This should be thick and almost stew-like — not soupy.

---

**Finisher — Bircher with Soy Yogurt and Berries** (~350 kcal · ~10g soy protein)

Rolled oats (45g), soy yogurt (150g), soy milk (100g), mixed berries (100g), ground flaxseed (12g), grated apple (60g). Assembled the night before.

## Considered Indulgences

These are the foods absent from the base plan. Costs are honest.

**Freely added — these improve the plan:**
- Extra raw vegetable volume at any course
- Extra berries in the finisher
- Kimchi or plain sauerkraut (30g) alongside the stir-fry or chili: ~10 kcal, microbiome benefit

**Once or twice a week — modest cost:**
- Dark chocolate, 85%+ (15–20g): ~90–120 kcal. At this percentage it is a health food — polyphenols, magnesium, modest blood pressure benefit. Have it after the finisher.
- One slice rye sourdough (60g) with the ribollita on Sunday: ~130 kcal, GI ~50. The most defensible bread option.
- Feta (15g) crumbled into the Wednesday salad: ~55 kcal, modest saturated fat and sodium.

**Occasional — once a week, with awareness:**
- One whole egg (hard-boiled) in the Tuesday Niçoise: +70 kcal, ~186mg dietary cholesterol. One per week is unlikely to materially affect LDL.
- A glass of red wine (150ml) with Saturday's tagine: ~125 kcal. The polyphenol evidence has weakened under scrutiny, but one glass weekly is not working against any of the stated goals.

**Avoid even occasionally:**
- Protein powder close to sleep — insulin spike at peak melatonin
- Any refined wheat, sugary drinks, processed meat
- Fruit juice
